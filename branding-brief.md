# Magnolia Made Cookies — Branding Brief

---

## 1. Name Assessment

### "Magnolia Made Cookies" — Verdict: ✅ Keep It

**Strengths:**
- *Magnolia* is a beautiful, evocative word — soft, Southern-gentle, floral, timeless. It signals warmth, grace, and natural beauty without being overdone.
- *Made* is the best possible verb here. It implies handcraft, intentionality, love. "Made by hand." It's honest and grounding.
- The alliteration of M-M-C is subtle but memorable — reads well on packaging, says well in conversation.
- It doesn't over-explain (not "Maya's Custom Sugar Cookies") — it has room to grow into a brand.
- Connecticut has a quiet, aspirational charm. A magnolia blooming in a New England yard is poetic.

**Potential Considerations:**
- Magnolia Bakery (NYC) is a well-known brand — not in the same space geographically or product-wise, but worth being aware of. Differentiation is easy given the custom, artisanal, home-kitchen positioning.
- The name doesn't scream "sugar cookies" specifically — but that's actually good. It gives flexibility as the brand evolves.

**Alternative Options (if she wants to explore):**
- **Made by Maya** — more personal, immediately human, very warm. Good if she wants her name front and center.
- **Alex & Eliza's Bakehouse** — charming if she wants to put the kids forward. Tells a family story instantly.
- **Magnolia & Co.** — slightly more neutral/expandable.
- **The Magnolia Kitchen** (note: there's a popular NZ baker with this name — avoid)
- **Maya Made** — elegant, even simpler. Works beautifully.

**Recommendation:** Stick with *Magnolia Made Cookies* as the full legal/display name. Consider *Magnolia Made* as the short form logo treatment.

## 2. Brand DNA / Vibe

**The One-Sentence Brief:**
> Magnolia Made is a Connecticut home bakery making heirloom-quality sugar cookies — the kind that feel like a warm hug, look like a piece of art, and taste like your grandmother's kitchen on a Sunday morning.

**Mood Words:**
Gentle · Handcrafted · Warm · Tender · Seasonal · Botanical · Home · Celebration · Artisanal · Quiet Luxury

**The Visual World:**
Think: afternoon light through linen curtains. A farmhouse table covered in flour. Flowers from the backyard in a small clay vase. A plate of cookies still warm from the oven. Someone's grandmother's china. A child's handwritten recipe card.

This is *pastry shop meets garden party meets Connecticut farmhouse kitchen.*

## 3. Logo Concept

### Primary Mark
**Style:** Delicate line-art illustration paired with refined serif typography.

**Illustration Element:** A magnolia blossom — either a single open flower or a sprig with 2-3 blooms and leaves. The illustration should feel hand-drawn, botanically accurate but not clinical — like something from a Victorian-era botanical notebook, with a modern softness to it. Fine, confident strokes. Nothing cartoony.

**Typography:**
- Brand name set in a refined serif with a slightly editorial quality — something between a wedding invitation and a bakery sign
- *"Magnolia Made"* as the primary lockup in the serif
- *"Cookies"* or *"Connecticut"* as a small secondary line in a lighter weight or a complementary script/italic
- Consider a badge/seal format (circular or oval) or a horizontal lockup

**Logo Variations to Design:**
1. **Full mark** — illustration + full text stacked
2. **Horizontal lockup** — illustration left, text right
3. **Icon only** — just the magnolia bloom (for favicons, profile pictures, stickers)
4. **Text only** — wordmark for situations where illustration doesn't reproduce cleanly

**Feel Reference:** Anthropologie packaging meets a Connecticut farmstand sign.

## 5. Typography

### Headline Font
**[Playfair Display](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Playfair+Display)** — Google Fonts
- Elegant, editorial serif. High contrast strokes. Feels like a magazine header or a bakery chalkboard sign gone digital.
- Use for: page titles, section headings, hero tagline
- Weight: 400 (regular) for most uses, 700 (bold) for primary hero text
- Style tip: Consider italic variant for pull quotes and emphasis

### Body / Secondary Font
**[Lato](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Lato)** — Google Fonts
- Clean, humanist sans-serif. Warm without being precious. Reads beautifully at small sizes.
- Use for: body copy, form labels, nav items, captions
- Weights: 300 (light for captions), 400 (regular for body), 700 (bold for subheadings)

### Accent / Signature Font (Optional)
**[Dancing Script](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Dancing+Script)** — Google Fonts
- Flowing, friendly handwriting script. Use very sparingly — taglines, small decorative text, "handmade" feeling moments
- Do NOT use for body copy or long text. Accent only.

### Pairing In Practice:
```
PLAYFAIR DISPLAY — Big, beautiful, 48px+ headings
Lato 300/400 — Readable body, 16-18px
Dancing Script — Small decorative moments only
```

## 6. Photography Direction

### Style: Warm, Natural, Soft

**Lighting:**
- Always natural light. Window light preferred — soft, indirect, slightly diffused through white curtains or a sheer
- Golden hour (late afternoon) for warmth
- Avoid harsh direct sun (creates hard shadows), avoid artificial flash
- Aim for a slightly overexposed, airy feel — not dark or moody

**Backgrounds:**
- White marble with warm tones (not cold blue-gray marble)
- Weathered light wood (not dark walnut)
- Linen or cotton fabric — cream, white, soft sage
- The kitchen table itself — authentic, lived-in

**Styling Props:**
- Fresh flowers from the garden (peonies, garden roses, eucalyptus, small magnolia blooms)
- Vintage/antique cake stands, small ramekins, clay bowls
- Ribbon, twine, tissue paper in the brand palette
- Cookie cutters laid casually in frame
- Small bottles of vanilla, cinnamon sticks, almond flour
- Children's hands (Alex and Eliza) — genuine family moments
- Gingham cloth napkins in soft tones

**Composition:**
- Overhead (flat lay) is the hero format for cookie trays
- 45° angle shots for individual cookie portraits
- Detail close-ups of icing work — this is where the craft shows
- Always leave breathing room — negative space is your friend
- Rule of thirds, never centered grids

**Color Treatment:**
- Warm, light editing preset (not vsco-oversaturated)
- Slight warm tone (+5-10% warmth)
- Gently lifted shadows, pulled-back highlights
- Consistent look across all photos — one editing style

**What to Avoid:**
- Dark, moody, restaurant-style food photography
- Busy backgrounds
- Plastic surfaces
- Heavily filtered/preset looks
- Stock-photo energy

## 7. Brand Voice

### Personality Archetype
**The Warm Creative Mom** — Talented, genuine, slightly playful, zero pretension. She's not performing artistry — she just genuinely loves making beautiful things for people she cares about.

### Voice Pillars

**Warm** — Every message feels like it comes from a real person who bakes in her kitchen, not a marketing department.

**Personal** — She mentions her kids, her kitchen, the real story. First person. Specific details > generic claims.

**Celebratory** — Cookies are for moments. Birthdays, baby showers, weddings, Tuesday afternoons. She honors every occasion, big and small.

**Honest** — Small batch means limited. Custom means it takes time. She's upfront about this and frames it as a feature, not a flaw.

**Slightly Playful** — Not silly. Not "lol!" energy. But a light touch. A wink. Warmth with a small smile in it.

### Voice in Practice

**DO:**
- Use "I" and "we" (the we meaning Maya + her family)
- Mention specific details (the kids, the kitchen, the season)
- Express genuine joy about baking
- Be specific about what makes things special
- Use sensory language (scent, texture, appearance)
- Celebrate the customer's occasion

**DON'T:**
- Use corporate-speak ("artisanal experience," "curated offerings")
- Be overly sales-y or promotional
- Use exclamation points more than sparingly
- Appropriate trendy slang
- Sound like a Yelp listing

### Sample Voice Benchmark
> "I started baking these for Alex's kindergarten birthday and couldn't stop. There's something about sitting at this kitchen table, figuring out a new design while Eliza chatters about which sprinkles to use — that's where the magic is, honestly."

*That's the voice.* Specific. Warm. Real.
