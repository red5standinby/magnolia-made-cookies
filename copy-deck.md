### Post 2 — The Process

**Caption:**
People ask me: how long does it take to make a custom order?

The honest answer: longer than you'd think, and exactly right.

The dough gets made the day before — it needs to rest overnight. The next morning I roll and cut each cookie by hand. They bake, they cool. Then comes the part I love most: the icing.

Royal icing is its own whole world. Flood coats. Outline work. Detail piping. Colors mixed by eye until they're just right. Some cookies need 3-4 layers of icing, drying in between each pass.

A single dozen custom cookies can take most of a day to finish — and I wouldn't have it any other way.

When you open the box, I want you to be genuinely surprised. That's the goal every time. 🌿

## FAQ Answers

### Custom Orders

**Q: How do I place a custom order?**
A: Fill out the inquiry form on the website (or send a DM on Instagram) with your occasion, timeline, rough quantity, and any design ideas you have. I'll follow up within 1-2 business days to chat through the details, confirm pricing, and get things started. Don't worry if you're not sure about the design yet — that's what the conversation is for.

**Q: How far in advance do I need to order?**
A: For most custom orders, I ask for at least **1-2 weeks' notice** — this gives me time to plan the design, source any specialty supplies, and make sure your cookies get the attention they deserve. For large orders (72+ cookies) or very detailed designs, 3-4 weeks is better. The sooner the better, especially around holidays and spring/summer wedding season.

**Q: What's the minimum order size?**
A: For custom decorated sugar cookies, the minimum is typically **one dozen (12 cookies)**. This is mostly a practical thing — detailed royal icing work requires setup and prep that doesn't scale down well below 12. For gift boxes and simpler designs, I'm flexible — just ask.

**Q: Can I see design options before I commit?**
A: Absolutely. Once we've chatted about your vision, I can share design references, color options, or rough sketches before you finalize. I want you to love what you're getting.

**Q: Do you do corporate or bulk orders?**
A: Yes! I love doing larger orders for events, corporate gifting, and client appreciation boxes. Reach out with your timeline and quantity and we'll figure out what works.

---

### Shipping

**Q: Do you ship cookies?**
A: Shipping is coming soon! Right now I'm primarily serving Connecticut and surrounding areas with local pickup. If you're outside CT and want to inquire about shipping, reach out — I'm happy to chat about it for the right order.

**Q: How do you package orders?**
A: Cookies are individually wrapped and packed carefully in a sturdy box, with tissue paper and ribbon. They look as good when they arrive as when they left my kitchen. Gift packaging is included — no extra charge.

**Q: How long do the cookies stay fresh?**
A: Sugar cookies with royal icing stay fresh for **1-2 weeks** at room temperature in their sealed packaging. For longer freshness, they can be frozen in an airtight container for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature — they taste freshly baked.

---

### Local Pickup

**Q: Where do I pick up my order?**
A: I'm based in Connecticut. Exact pickup address is shared once your order is confirmed. Pickup is easy, convenient, and I'll have your box ready to go — no waiting around.

**Q: Can you deliver locally?**
A: For certain orders and certain areas, local delivery may be possible — ask when you inquire and I'll let you know what we can work out.

---

### Allergens

**Q: What allergens are in your cookies?**
A: My standard sugar cookies contain **wheat (gluten), eggs, dairy (butter, milk), and soy** (from some vanilla extracts and butter). I bake in a home kitchen that is not allergen-free. If you have a serious allergy, please let me know upfront so we can discuss whether my cookies are right for your order.

**Q: Do you make gluten-free or vegan cookies?**
A: Not currently, but this is something I'm exploring. If you have a specific dietary need, reach out — I may be able to accommodate or we can have an honest conversation about what's possible.

**Q: Do you use any artificial flavors or colors?**
A: My dough uses real butter, quality vanilla, and wholesome pantry ingredients. Icing colors are made with food-grade gel coloring — I don't use artificial flavors in the dough itself.
